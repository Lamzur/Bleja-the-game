Artwork

One of the most challenging things was to come up with an idea for the artstyle and remain original at the same time. We opted for a pixel theme since we liked the concept and it was our initial idea.

However, we didn't think it would be as hard, and soon were reminded that being an animator is hard work. Here is how it started:

This is the demo version of our character design.
So a couple of things to note:

  • It looks bland and ugly.
  • Not what we initially imagined.
  • Overall bad proportions.
  • Bad shading.

Finding what's wrong and fixing it was hard for someone new to this, but with a few tweaks here and there a new design was born, arguably even worse than the last.


The second (better)version.
As well as the last one, here's some things about it:

  • Shows more detail.
  • Better shading.
  • Still looks amateur.
  • Bad view angle.


Eventually we changed the game idea from top-down to 2D view. This was the turning point in the style since up untill now we focused on a single idea.

After a few attempts and many more scrap ideas that we didn't show you, we ended up with this:

Final .gif product of our hard work.

  • It looks simple yet "enough".
  • We liked it so much, we made an animation right after.
  • The style brings out our characteristics perfectly.

When we're finished with the drawing, we export the animation as a spritesheet.
We usually export it in a 4 - single frame images that animate again in our engine.

It just goes to show how much time something can take if you really put effort in.
When we started, we had little to none experience in this field, now we're off to drawing a full - on game.
